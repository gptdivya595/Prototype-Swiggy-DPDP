# Pain Points — Privacy & Consent Today

The friction users hit around privacy and consent in Swiggy's current experience, and where each maps to a DPDP gap.

## User-facing pain points

| # | Pain point | What happens today | DPDP principle broken |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Consent fatigue** | 4,000-word policy at signup → users abandon or blind-accept | Specific & Informed (S.6) |
| 2 | **Dark-pattern anxiety** | Pre-ticked marketing box, "Agree to continue" coercion | Affirmative action / Free consent (S.6) |
| 3 | **Vernacular gap** | Notices are English-only → excludes 60%+ non-English-first users | Notice in clear, plain language (S.5/S.7) |
| 4 | **No memory / no recall** | Users can't remember what they agreed to, nor revoke it easily | Right to withdraw (S.7) |
| 5 | **Parental blindspot** | Guardians can't review or authorize data collection for minors | Children's data & verifiable parental consent (S.9) |
| 6 | **Purpose creep** | Phone (for OTP) reused for marketing; address (for delivery) reused for geo-ads | Purpose limitation (S.5) |
| 7 | **Silent collection** | Background location + analytics start before first order | Data minimisation (S.4) |

## Current-state friction & risk points

- **Legal:** Data Protection Board can issue show-cause notices immediately on enforcement.
- **Conversion:** ~18% estimated drop-off at the "Terms wall" for new Tier-2/3 users.
- **Trust:** "Data privacy" is the #2 complaint among Swiggy One (premium) members in NPS surveys.
- **Competitive:** Zomato has already piloted granular consent toggles in select markets.

## Why these pains matter per segment

- **Priya (privacy-conscious urban diner):** poor consent UX = a reason to switch to Zomato.
- **Ramesh (low-literacy trust-first user):** silent churn after one confusing experience.
- **Ananya's guardian (child user):** uninstall + negative social post; largest legal exposure.

> The through-line: today the user is asked to trade *all* their data for *any* service, in a language and format they can't parse, with no way back. Every pain is simultaneously a **trust cost** and a **compliance breach**.
